Explanation:

According to the Bhagavad Gita, the three gunas—Sattva, Rajas, and Tamas—represent fundamental qualities that influence human behavior and consciousness.

Sattva:

Sattva is the mode of goodness and represents purity, harmony, and virtue. Individuals dominated by Sattva exhibit qualities such as wisdom, compassion, and a balanced mind. They tend to pursue spiritual growth and self-realization.

Rajas:

Rajas is the mode of passion and denotes activity, desire, and restlessness. Those influenced by Rajas are driven by ambition, achievement, and material pursuits. While Rajas can lead to success, it may also lead to attachment and ego.

Tamas:

Tamas is the mode of ignorance and signifies inertia, lethargy, and delusion. Individuals in the grip of Tamas may exhibit laziness, ignorance, and a lack of clarity. Breaking free from Tamas requires conscious effort towards self-improvement.

Lord Krishna in the Gita advises individuals to strive for a balance of the gunas, emphasizing the cultivation of Sattva for spiritual evolution.
